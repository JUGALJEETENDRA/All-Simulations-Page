📘 Strategic Reference Board: Strategic Analysis (Module 5.2)

1. SWOT Analysis: Helps evaluate internal capabilities (Strengths, Weaknesses) alongside external forces (Opportunities, Threats) to structure resource positioning.

2. Demand Analysis: Measures client intent, volume demands, price sensitivity, and requirements to validate market size and launch trajectories.

3. Competitor Analysis: Analyzes competitor features, pricing models, market segments, and capabilities to determine differentiation gaps and defense loops.

4. Environmental Scanning: Unites multiple dimensions (SWOT, Competitors, Customer demand) to form stable business models.

          🏛️ Analyst Reference Manual
          SWOT Analysis Framework:

          Useful to identify internal capabilities (strengths and weaknesses) and external factors (opportunities and threats). Strengths and opportunities represent helpful drivers, while weaknesses and threats identify risks to mitigate.

          Market Demand Analysis:

          Helps strategic expansion by evaluating demographics (population, purchasing power) to find targets that maximize customer volume while minimizing operating risk levels.

          Competitor Benchmarking:

          Compares Price, Quality, and Features to find competitive gaps. Differentiation models leverage quality and feature sets to bypass direct price wars.

          Blueprint Integration:

          Translates high-level assessments into actionable operations, balancing business growth vs. risk vs. unique advantages.
